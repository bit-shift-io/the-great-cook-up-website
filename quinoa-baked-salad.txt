a:T6ae,<h1>Quinoa Baked Salad</h1>
<p>Serves 6
Prep 20 min
Cook 35 min</p>
<h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul>
<li>1 carrot, peeled, cut into matchsticks </li>
<li>½ red onion, thinly sliced</li>
<li>1 cup (190g) tricolour quinoa </li>
<li>16 head cauliflower, cut into florets </li>
<li>1 medium gold sweet potato, peeled, chopped</li>
<li>1 red capsicum, seeded, thinly sliced</li>
<li>1 tbs sunflower oil</li>
<li>2 tsp curry powder</li>
<li>½ cup (70g) slivered almonds</li>
<li>400g can chickpeas, rinsed, drained</li>
<li>½ cup (80g) currants </li>
<li>2 celery sticks, thinly sliced </li>
<li>½ cup flat-leaf parsley leaves </li>
<li>60g pkt Coles Australian Baby Spinach</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dressing</h2>
<ul>
<li>¼ cup (60ml) lemon juice </li>
<li>1 tbs wholegrain mustard </li>
<li>2 tbs maple syrup</li>
<li>1/4 cup (60ml) sunflower oil</li>
<li>Honey &amp; Mustard dressing (optional from the shop)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Method</h2>
<ol>
<li>Cook the quinoa in a saucepan of boiling water (~1 cup quinoa, 2 cup water) till no water left.</li>
<li>Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper. </li>
<li>Toss the cauliflower, sweet potato, capsicum, oil and curry powder in a bowl to coat. Bake for 25-30 mins or until tender (preboil is an idea too). </li>
<li>Add almonds and roast for a further 5 mins or until toasted.</li>
<li>To make lemon-mustard dressing, place the oil, lemon juice, mustard and maple syrup in a screw-top jar and shake.</li>
<li>Combine quinoa, cauliflower mixture, chickpeas, currants, celery, parsley, spinach, onion and carrot in a serving bowl. Drizzle with dressing to serve.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Notes</h2>
<ul>
<li>Honey &amp; Mustard dressing (optional from the shop)</li>
</ul>
