a:T6e2,<h1>Cheesy Beef and Lentil Meatballs</h1>
<p>Serves 10?</p>
<p>Time: ?</p>
<h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul>
<li>1 kilo lean beef mince</li>
<li>800 g can brown lentils, (did not rinse and drain them like the recipe says)</li>
<li>1 red onion, finely chopped</li>
<li>2 zucchini, coarsely grated</li>
<li>2/3 cup fresh basil leaves substituted for dried basil leaves</li>
<li>50 g (2/4 cup) quinoa flakes</li>
<li>4 tsp minced garlic</li>
<li>2 eggs, lightly whisked (can leave this out if you want as not making meatballs)</li>
<li>4 tsp dried oregano leaves</li>
<li>4 tsp olive oil
*4 cups woolworths essentials chunky pasta sauce</li>
<li>400 g cherry tomatoes, halved</li>
<li>4 tsp balsamic vinegar</li>
<li>80 g (2/3 cup) coarsely grated fresh mozzarella </li>
<li>150 g greaak feta, crumbled</li>
</ul>
<h2>Method</h2>
<ol>
<li>Place the mince, lentils, onion, zucchini, basil, quinoa flakes, garlic, egg (if you want) and oregano in a large bowl. Season well with salt and pepper. Use clean hands to mix well until combined.</li>
<li>Heat the oil in a large wok over a medium-high heat. Cook the meat etc that was placed together in a large bowl until mince meat is brown.</li>
<li>Add woolworths essentials chunky pasta sauce to the wok.</li>
<li>Combine tomato and vinegar in a bowl. Season. Add the tomato mixture to the wok. Add the mozarella and feta to the wok. Simmer for a few minutes.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Notes</h2>
<p>Original recipe is too slow and time consuming so I turned it into a chunky meat sauce.
Can have each serve with half a tub of brown rice. </p>
