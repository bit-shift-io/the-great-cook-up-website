a:Ta6b,<h1>Beef and Broccoli</h1>
<p>Serves 1</p>
<h2>Marinade</h2>
<ul>
<li>200g of beef (I’m using chuck tender)</li>
<li>1 Tb of Chinese cooking wine</li>
<li>2 tsp of light soy sauce</li>
<li>1 tsp of cornstarch</li>
<li>1/2 tsp of black pepper</li>
<li>1/4 tsp of baking soda</li>
<li>1 tsp of oil</li>
</ul>
<h2>Sauce</h2>
<ul>
<li>1/2 Tb oyster sauce</li>
<li>1/2 Tb hoisin</li>
<li>1 Tb light soy sauce</li>
<li>3 Tb water </li>
<li>2 tsp cornstarch</li>
<li>1 tsp sugar</li>
<li>1 tsp pepper to taste</li>
</ul>
<h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul>
<li>1 cup of broccoli</li>
<li>1 Tb of minced garlic</li>
</ul>
<h2>Extras</h2>
<ul>
<li>bok choy</li>
<li>cellary</li>
<li>onion</li>
<li>cashew nuts</li>
</ul>
<h2>Method</h2>
<ol>
<li>Slice your beef into thin pieces. I am using chuck tender. Make sure you cut against the fiber. This way, your beef will be less chewy.</li>
<li>Marinate it with 1 Tb of Chinese cooking wine, 2 tsp of light soy sauce, 1 tsp of cornstarch, some freshly ground pepper and 1/4 tsp of baking soda. This is what Chinese restaurants do to tenderize the beef- especially if they are using a tough cut of beef. Without baking soda, it’s gonna be a disaster.</li>
<li>After it is well combined. We make the sauce. 1 Tb of light soy sauce, 1 Tb of oyster sauce, 2-3 Tb of water, 2 tsp of cornstarch, some black pepper</li>
<li>Cut the broccoli into bite sizes. Blanch it in boiling water for 1 or 2 minutes. Don’t cover it or else the broccoli will lose its green color. Turn off the heat and put it into ice water for 3-5 minutes to stop the cooking process. Take it out and shake off the excess water. Using this technique, the broccoli will stay fresh and crunchy. </li>
<li>Next, fry the beef. Heat up about 1 Tb of oil. Give it a toss so the bottom is coated nicely.  </li>
<li>Add in the beef. Try to separate it so most of the beef touches the bottom of the wok. Let one side fry for 15-20 seconds. Use your spatula to flip it over and fry the other side for 15-20 seconds. You should also be able to smell the scent of the smoky beef. So good. Take it out and set it aside. </li>
<li>In the same wok, add a little bit of oil. Put in some garlic and give it a stir. Pour the sauce into the hot wok. It will start thickening quickly. Then add in the broccoli. Mix it a little bit. Add the beef back into the wok. Coat it nicely. You are done.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Notes</h2>
<ul>
<li>Use extra water if not using broccoli</li>
</ul>
