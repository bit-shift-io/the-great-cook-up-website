a:T752,<h1>Banana Muffins</h1>
<p>serves 12</p>
<h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul>
<li>1 and 1/2 cups (188g) all-purpose flour or whole wheat flour </li>
<li>1 teaspoon baking powder</li>
<li>1 teaspoon baking soda</li>
<li>1/2 teaspoon salt</li>
<li>1 teaspoon ground cinnamon</li>
<li>1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg</li>
<li>3 large ripe bananas (about 1 and 1/2 cups mashed)</li>
<li>6 Tablespoons (85g) unsalted butter, melted (or melted coconut oil)</li>
<li>2/3 cup (135g) packed light or dark brown sugar </li>
<li>1 large egg</li>
<li>1 teaspoon vanilla extract</li>
<li>2 Tablespoons (30ml) milk or sour cream</li>
</ul>
<h2>Extras</h2>
<ul>
<li>up to 1 cup extras chocolate chips, oats, nuts</li>
</ul>
<h2>Method</h2>
<ol>
<li>Preheat  oven to  220 C</li>
<li>Spray or grease a muffin pan or use cupcake liners</li>
<li>Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg together in a large bowl. Set aside. </li>
<li>mash the bananas. </li>
<li>whisk the melted butter, brown sugar, egg, vanilla extract, and milk.</li>
<li>Mix in the bananas</li>
<li>Add to the dry ingredients then beat or whisk until combined. If adding nuts or chocolate chips, fold them in now. Batter will be thick.</li>
<li>Spoon the batter into muffin pan or liners,  filling them all the way to the top. Bake for 5 minutes at 220C then reduce the oven temperature to 180C </li>
<li>Bake for an additional 15 -18 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. The total time these muffins take in the oven is about 20 - 25 minutes, give or take. For mini muffins, bake 12-14 total minutes at  180C. </li>
<li>Allow the muffins to cool for 5 minutes in the muffin pan, then transfer to a wire rack to continue cooling.</li>
<li>Muffins stay fresh stored at room temperature for a few days or in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. You can freeze them :)</li>
</ol>
